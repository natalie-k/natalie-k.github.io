Goal 3: Modernization of Pidgin

Pidgin is deeply rooted in Hawaiian history. It emerged in Hawaii’s sugar cane plantations in the mid to late 1800s. It’s said that it started as a way for plantation workers to communicate with each other because of their diverse languages (including Hawaiian, Japanese, Cantonese, Portuguese, and English).

We’re creating HIbridgin as a modern form of Pidgin. HIbridgin will be a mix of Pidgin, English, and other forms of communication used today, such as emojis and abbreviations.

We hope to encourage people to learn more about Pidgin and it’s rich and controversial history by including Pidgin and HIbridgin taglines on our merchandise.
